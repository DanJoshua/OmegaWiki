## Overview

Self-evolving agents are LLM-based agents whose behavior is not fixed at deployment: they accumulate experience, refine memory, update tools or skills, and adapt their policies during operation. The area sits at the intersection of agentic systems, continual learning, and tool-use, and is currently developing rapidly.

This Summary tracks ΩmegaWiki's coverage of the area, seeded by `/init` for the topic "Self-evolving Agents" on 2026-05-02. Concrete claims, mechanisms, and benchmarks are linked from individual paper pages once ingested.

## Core areas

- Test-time learning and memory evolution
- Experience-driven policy updates and lifecycle loops
- Tool-use and skill acquisition under self-modification
- Agentic search, depth/width benchmarking, and information seeking
- Surveys characterizing what/when/how/where agents evolve
